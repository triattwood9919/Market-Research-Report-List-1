<p><strong>The Protein Sequencing Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Edman Degradation</li><li>Mass Spectrometry</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The protein sequencing market primarily features two key types: Edman degradation and mass spectrometry. Edman degradation involves systematically removing amino acids from a protein's N-terminus, enabling the determination of its sequence. In contrast, mass spectrometry analyzes the mass-to-charge ratio of ionized protein fragments, providing rapid, high-throughput sequencing capabilities. Both methods offer unique advantages, with Edman being suitable for shorter peptides, while mass spectrometry excels in handling complex mixtures and larger proteins, driving advancements in proteomics and biotechnology.</p></p>
