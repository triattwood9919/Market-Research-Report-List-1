<p><p>A Vehicle Bias Tire is a type of tire construction where the tire's plies are layered diagonally across each other, typically at a 30 to 40-degree angle. <p><p>The Vehicle Bias Tire market includes two main types: Ordinary Bias Tires and Bias Belted Tires. Ordinary Bias Tires feature crisscrossed ply layers at an angle, offering durability and load-bearing capacity, commonly used in off-road and heavy-duty vehicles. Bias Belted Tires add a stabilizing belt beneath the tread, enhancing stability, speed, and tread life, making them suitable for passenger cars and light trucks. Both types cater to specific vehicle needs, balancing performance, durability, and cost-effectiveness in diverse driving conditions.</p></p>
