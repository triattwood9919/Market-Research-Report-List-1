<p><p>Digital Holographic Microscopy (DHM) is an advanced imaging technique that captures the three-dimensional structure of specimens through the interference of light waves. It allows for label-free observation, making it ideal for live cell imaging and biological samples. The market is segmented into two main types: the Label-Free Interferometric Technique, which provides high-resolution imaging without staining or labeling, and the Quantitative Phase Imaging market, which quantifies phase shifts to obtain detailed information about sample morphology and dynamics.</p></p>
