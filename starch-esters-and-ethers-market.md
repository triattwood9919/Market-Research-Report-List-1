<p><p>Starch esters and ethers are modified starch derivatives created through chemical reactions that improve their functional properties. Starch esters are produced by esterifying starch with fatty acids, imparting enhanced properties like emulsification and thickeners. Starch ethers, on the other hand, are formed by reacting starch with alkyl groups, resulting in compounds that exhibit improved solubility, stability, and film-forming abilities. These modified starches find applications across various industries, including food, pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and adhesives.</p><p>The market for starch esters and ethers is witnessing significant growth, driven by increasing demand for clean-label products and natural additives in the food industry.
