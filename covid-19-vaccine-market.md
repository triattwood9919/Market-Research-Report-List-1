<p><p>The COVID-19 vaccine market includes various types based on technology. **DNA vaccines** use genetic material to trigger immune responses. **Non-replicating viral vector vaccines** employ modified viruses to deliver genetic instructions without replicating. **Protein subunit vaccines** use harmless pieces of the virus to stimulate immunity. **RNA vaccines** deliver messenger RNA to produce viral proteins, prompting an immune response. **Others** include inactivated or live-attenuated vaccines. Each type targets the virus differently, offering diverse approaches to immunization and addressing varying safety, efficacy, and production needs.</p></p>
