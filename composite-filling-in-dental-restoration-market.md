<p><p>Composite filling in dental restoration refers to the use of tooth-colored resin materials to repair cavities, cracks, or other dental damage. Additionally, the growing prevalence of dental caries and the aging population are fueling market expansion.</p><p>Latest trends in the market include the development of nano-hybrid and bulk-fill composite materials, which enhance durability and reduce application time. Digital dentistry and 3D printing are also revolutionizing the fabrication of composite restorations, improving precision and customization. Furthermore, the adoption of bioactive composites that promote remineralization and reduce secondary caries is gaining traction. These innovations, coupled with increasing dental tourism and insurance coverage, are expected to sustain market growth in the coming years.</p></p>
